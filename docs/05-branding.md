## 3. Top Recommendation

### Name: **Pavo**
### Domain: `pavo.app`
### Estimated cost: ~$13/yr

**Why Pavo:**

**1. No product conflicts.** At time of writing (March 2026), no significant AI product, Chrome extension, or developer tool uses the name Pavo. Zero trademark collision risk. The only Pavo entries are a defunct iOS app and a constellation — neither relevant.

**2. Phonetics are clean.** Two syllables (PAH-voh). Ends in a vowel — open, memorable, easy to say in a podcast or demo. Similar to how "Figma," "Vercel," "Notion" are easy to say without explanation.

**3. The brand story is there without forcing it.** Pavo is Latin for peacock — a bird known for its vivid display and intelligence. A browser agent that surfaces the web's intelligence to you. The "display" metaphor works doubly: the side panel *displays* information. You don't have to lead with this backstory; it's there if someone asks.

**4. Aesthetic fit.** "Pavo" sounds like what a serious developer would name a serious tool. It doesn't scream "AI!" like names ending in -Bot or -AI do. It ages well.

**5. Domain portfolio.** `pavo.app` + `pavo.dev` both available. Both under $15/yr each. Register both immediately to protect the brand.

**6. Icon scales.** A geometric peacock eye (concentric circles, an iris-like mark) reads clearly at 16px, 48px, and 128px without becoming illegible.

---

**Runner-up: Wren** (`wren.run` — ~$5/yr)

Best if you want the cheapest possible domain and enjoy the "name.run = imperative sentence" cleverness. `wren.run` is a URL that reads as a command. Lower cost than `pavo.app` but slightly less premium-feeling.

**Runner-up: Browe** (`browe.app` — ~$13/yr)

Best if you want the name to immediately communicate "browsing." The intentional misspelling is a clever signal to developers. Slightly less elegant but very clear in purpose.

## 4. Logo Concepts

The extension icon must work at three Chrome-mandated sizes: **16px** (toolbar), **48px** (Extensions page), and **128px** (Chrome Web Store). Design at 128px first; then derive the 16px and 48px variants — never just scale down.

---

### 4.1 Pavo

**Concept: Geometric peacock eye — an iris/lens mark**

The peacock's tail feather eyespot is a perfect icon: circular, distinctive, recognizable even when simplified to 3–4 concentric elements. It reads as an eye, a lens, a viewport — all relevant metaphors for a browser agent.

**Color palette:**
- Background: `#0A0A0F` (near-black, cool undertone)
- Outer ring: `#0D7A8A` (deep teal — intelligence, depth)
- Inner ring: `#1BA8BF` (lighter teal — glass-like luminosity)
- Center dot: `#C8A84B` (iridescent gold — the peacock's shimmer)
- Light text / wordmark: `#F5F4F0`

**At 128px:**
- Rounded square container, `border-radius: 22%`, fill `#0A0A0F`
- Outer circle: `cx=64, cy=64, r=44`, fill none, stroke `#0D7A8A`, stroke-width `3`
- Middle circle: `cx=64, cy=64, r=30`, fill none, stroke `#1BA8BF`, stroke-width `2`
- Inner circle: `cx=64, cy=64, r=16`, fill `#1BA8BF`, opacity `0.3`
- Center dot: `cx=64, cy=64, r=8`, fill `#C8A84B`
- Optional: 6 radial lines from r=30 to r=44 at 60° intervals, stroke `#0D7A8A`, stroke-width `1`, opacity `0.4` — suggests feather barbs subtly

**At 48px:**
- Same structure, simplified: outer ring + inner ring + center dot only. No radial lines.
- Stroke widths reduced by ~50%.

**At 16px:**
- Drop all rings. Single filled circle `r=5`, fill `#C8A84B` (gold dot) centered in a `#0A0A0F` rounded square.
- The gold dot on dark background reads instantly as a favicon-scale mark.

**SVG description (128px, developer-ready):**
```xml
<!-- Container: 128x128 rounded square -->
<rect width="128" height="128" rx="28" ry="28" fill="#0A0A0F"/>

<!-- Outer ring -->
<circle cx="64" cy="64" r="44" fill="none" stroke="#0D7A8A" stroke-width="3"/>

<!-- Feather barb lines (optional, 6 lines at 60° intervals) -->
<line x1="64" y1="20" x2="64" y2="34" stroke="#0D7A8A" stroke-width="1" opacity="0.4" transform="rotate(0 64 64)"/>
<line x1="64" y1="20" x2="64" y2="34" stroke="#0D7A8A" stroke-width="1" opacity="0.4" transform="rotate(60 64 64)"/>
<line x1="64" y1="20" x2="64" y2="34" stroke="#0D7A8A" stroke-width="1" opacity="0.4" transform="rotate(120 64 64)"/>
<line x1="64" y1="20" x2="64" y2="34" stroke="#0D7A8A" stroke-width="1" opacity="0.4" transform="rotate(180 64 64)"/>
<line x1="64" y1="20" x2="64" y2="34" stroke="#0D7A8A" stroke-width="1" opacity="0.4" transform="rotate(240 64 64)"/>
<line x1="64" y1="20" x2="64" y2="34" stroke="#0D7A8A" stroke-width="1" opacity="0.4" transform="rotate(300 64 64)"/>

<!-- Middle ring -->
<circle cx="64" cy="64" r="30" fill="none" stroke="#1BA8BF" stroke-width="2"/>

<!-- Inner glow fill -->

```

### 4.2 Wren

**Concept: Abstract W-mark or minimal bird silhouette**

At large sizes, a wren in profile — small, upright, tail raised. Reduced to ~7 anchor points: not a realistic illustration, but a clean geometric mark. At 16px, an abstract W formed by two inward V-strokes.

**Color palette:**
- Background: `#0A0A0A` (near-black)
- Bird / W-mark: `#FFFFFF` (white)
- Accent (eye dot at 48px+): `#6EE7B7` (emerald-mint — a single breath of color)
- Glow at 128px: `rgba(110, 231, 183, 0.12)` soft outer bloom on the eye

**At 128px:**
- Rounded square container, fill `#0A0A0A`
- Bird silhouette: smooth closed path, facing right, spanning approx x=28–100, y=35–95
  - Beak tip at (100, 58), breast curves down to (85, 90), belly to (55, 95), back to (30, 52), crown to (58, 35), back to beak
  - Fill `#FFFFFF`
- Upswept tail stroke: (55, 95) to (35, 75), stroke `#FFFFFF`, stroke-width 3, round cap
- Eye: `cx=78, cy=52, r=3`, fill `#6EE7B7`
- Optional wing hint: single curve from (45, 62) to (72, 58), stroke `rgba(255,255,255,0.35)`, stroke-width 1.5

**At 48px:**
- Simplified bird silhouette — less point count, same body shape
- Eye dot: `cx` proportional, `r=2`, fill `#6EE7B7`

**At 16px:**
- Abstract W-mark only (bird is illegible at this size):
  - Line 1: (3,5) → (6,11) → (8,7)
  - Line 2: (8,7) → (10,11) → (13,5)
  - Stroke `#FFFFFF`, stroke-width 1.5, linecap round, linejoin round
  - No fill

**SVG description (16px variant, developer-ready):**
```xml
<rect width="16" height="16" rx="3" ry="3" fill="#0A0A0A"/>
<polyline
  points="3,5 6,11 8,7 10,11 13,5"
  fill="none"
  stroke="#FFFFFF"
  stroke-width="1.5"
  stroke-linecap="round"
  stroke-linejoin="round"
/>
```

### 4.3 Sift

**Concept: Grid of dots — one highlighted dot signals the found result**

A 4×4 grid of small circles. One dot (typically bottom-right) is filled with accent color. The rest are dim. Evokes scanning, filtering, extracting signal from noise — exactly the agent's behavior when reading a web page.

**Color palette:**
- Background: `#0F0F0F`
- Inactive dots: `#2D2D2D` (dark gray — the noise)
- Active dot (found): `#2dd4bf` (teal — peacock-themed, distinctive in AI space)
- Active dot glow: `rgba(45, 212, 191, 0.25)`, r=12 behind the active dot

**At 128px:**
- Rounded square container, fill `#0F0F0F`
- 4×4 grid, centered. Grid origin: top-left at (22, 22), dot spacing 28px, dot radius 7
- All 16 dots: fill `#2D2D2D`
- Highlight the 4th column, 4th row dot (position index [3][3]): `cx=106, cy=106`
  - fill `#2dd4bf`
  - Glow ring: `cx=106, cy=106, r=12`, fill none, stroke `#2dd4bf`, stroke-width 1.5, opacity 0.3

**At 48px:**
- 3×3 grid, 5px radius dots, 14px spacing. Active dot at [2][2].

**At 16px:**
- 2×2 grid only, 2px radius dots, 5px spacing. Active dot at [1][1] (bottom-right).
- Grid centered in container.

## 5. Chrome Web Store Listing Name

Chrome Web Store extension names have a **75-character hard limit**. The listing name appears in search results, the Extensions page, and the toolbar tooltip. It must be descriptive enough for organic search while being clean enough for returning users.

### Recommended (for Pavo):

> **`Pavo — AI Web Agent: Browse, Click & Chat from Your Side Panel`**
> 63 characters — within limit.

Keywords covered: AI agent, browse, click, chat, side panel.

## Appendix: TLD Notes

- **`.run`** is the hidden gem of this research. At ~$3–6/yr (Porkbun), it is the cheapest viable developer-friendly TLD. When the product name is an action word or a noun that implies action, the `.run` TLD creates a double meaning: `wren.run`, `sift.run`, `navi.run`, `pavo.run`. All read as URLs and as commands.
- **`.dev`** is the most credible TLD for developer tools that cannot get `.com`. It is operated by Google, requires HTTPS by default (HSTS preloaded), and signals developer-first positioning clearly.
- **`.app`** is slightly more consumer/product-facing than `.dev`. Good if the product is intended for a broader audience (not just developers).
- **`.so`** costs ~$50–100/yr (Somalia ccTLD). Despite the appeal, it exceeds the $20 annual budget significantly.
- **`.sh`** costs ~$30–50/yr (St Helena ccTLD). Great aesthetics for shell-adjacent tools but not budget-friendly.
- **`.io`** runs ~$30–60/yr in 2026. Skip unless the project is funded and brand identity demands it.
- **`.ai`** is ~$80–150/yr. Not in scope at the stated budget.
